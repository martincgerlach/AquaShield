Before a tsunami

Build your kit first

  • Keep water, medicine, and a flashlight in one bag.
  • Save emergency numbers in your phone and on paper.
  • Agree on one meeting point with your family.

During a tsunami

Move fast, stay clear

  • Leave the coast and go to higher ground immediately.
  • Do not walk or drive through floodwater.
  • Keep listening to local alerts and instructions.

After a tsunami

Return carefully

  • Wait for officials to say the area is safe.
  • Stay away from damaged roads, wires, and dirty water.
  • Check your plan and contact your family again.

Common tsunami questions

This section gives users quick answers on the front page before they move to the full guide or make a personal plan.

What is the first thing I should do?

Move away from the coast and go to higher ground immediately. Do not wait to collect extra things if there is a real warning.

What should be in my emergency kit?

Keep water, medication, a flashlight, a phone charger, and important contact information ready in one easy-to-carry bag.

Can I drive through floodwater?

No. Floodwater can hide damage, carry strong force, and block roads. It is safer to stay away and follow official evacuation routes.

When is it safe to return home?

Return only when local authorities say the area is safe. Water, damaged roads, and electrical dangers may still be present after the first wave is over.

⚠ Always follow local authorities’ instructions first.
